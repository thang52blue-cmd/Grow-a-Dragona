# Product Context (foundation)

> Seeded 2026-07-14 from `Doc/Grow_a_Dragona_GDD.txt`. Do not edit without explicit human
> instruction (AGENTS.md foundation-file rule).

## Why this exists

GDD vision: build a lovable dragon IP, not just a Roblox game about dragons — one that can extend
across games/media/toys long-term. The immediate bet (GDD §1.1) is that combining DragonVale's
raise-and-collect loop with Grow a Garden's idle-RNG loop, in 3D, with a real-time social raid layer,
is an underserved combination on Roblox.

## Players

- Target audience: ages 8-16, casual and mid-core Roblox players (GDD field table).
- Session design (GDD §2.2):
  - Quick session (5-15 min): collect eggs, sell, buy 1-3 gacha pulls, feed dragons.
  - Deep session (30-60+ min): expand plot, manage element synergies, defend raids, save for rares.
- Key design goal: a new player reaches their first gacha hatching egg purchase within 5-15 minutes
  of joining. That first pull is the retention hook.

## What makes it distinct (USP, GDD §1.3)

The only idle game on Roblox where players watch dragons grow and produce eggs to buy rarer ones,
while other players can try to steal those eggs at night.

## Monetization stance (GDD §7.1)

Free-to-play: all core progression must be reachable without spending. Robux accelerates time-to-
rare-dragon, it does not gate access to content. This constrains gacha odds and any pay-only path —
a Robux-only path to a dragon that gold cannot eventually reach would break the stated design intent.
